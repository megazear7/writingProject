What Can Green Energy Do?

With the development of sustainable energy (which is also called renewable energy or green energy), Green energy play an increasingly important role in our daily life. So far there are three-generation technologies

  1. First generation: Hydroelectric power, geothermal power and biomass briquettes. The first generation contains least technological elements and is usually applied on large scale like dam and biomass reaction plant. For homeowners and small business owners, it is not practical in term of availability.
  2. Second generation: Solar energy, wind energy and ethanol. (The stage we are in now). The second generation is the technology that is most widely used so far. Solar panel and turbine are two most common technologies.
  3. Third generation (which are not yet widely demonstrated): Bio-fuels, ocean tide energy and “Artificial leaf”, which can break water down to oxygen and hydrogen molecules. Third generation is focus on solving the problems that second generation technologies have and develop new green energy resources to meet the huge demand of use of green energy.

But There Are Some Problems...

Problems With Green Energy

High cost and maintenance fee. For example, depending the different size of solar panels, the cost is ranged from 3500 dollars to 20000 dollars excluding the installation fee and maintenance fee. Within the lifetime of a solar panel, the maintenance fee is about 10% to 15% of its cost. Compared to the return of investment on solar panel, install solar panel with high cost and low return of investment is not a practical and ideal choice.
Place limitation. The access to green energy is another problem homeowners and small business may face. People live in city may have no place to install the green energy collection and restoration equipment like solar panel or turbine because high and dense building block the sunshine and wind at the most of time. Only citizens live in countryside or plain are equipped the natural conditions to install green energy equipment.
The economic effect of use of green energy. Economic input of is greater than output, the installation cost and maintenance fee is way higher than the money saving from use of green energy. Take America for example, the annual electricity expense per person is 120-240 dollars. Within average lifetime of a solar panel, which is five years. The normal electricity expense is much lower than the all solar panel relevant fee minus the saving from use of solar panel.

There Are Solutions!

Solutions To Those Problems

Install solar panel or turbines in a whole community rather single house to reduce cost and improve the efficiency. In a village in Jamaica, whole village install a set of solar panels. By doing this way, the average expense for every family is much lower and overall efficiency is improved a lot. This is a valid solution to high installation expense.
Remote solar panel. Remote solar panel is a best alternative so far. On the one hand, price of remote solar panel is below 1000 dollars, which are lower than traditional solar panels. On the other hand, remote solar panel can improve the efficiency. In the morning, remote solar panel can face toward the east side. And in the afternoon, you can adjust the position of solar panel by putting solar panel face the west side.

But There Are Some Problems Without Solutions

The Future Of Green Energy For You

With the development of third generation, the current problems can be solved. First, scientists are developing a new synthetic silicon panel. New silicon panel will lower the cost (even the remote solar panel) and can be widely used by homeowners and small business owners.
Second, new green energy resources will provide people with more available and economic access to green energy. For example, "Artificial Leaf" is a brand new technology, which is able to split water into hydrogen and oxygen directly from solar power when dropped into a glass of water and release huge amount of chemical energy. Within the equipment, a bubble of hydrogen is generated from one side of the "Artificial Leaf", while the other side produces bubbles of oxygen.
